The anticipated results

This is also the follow-up of the ICI(immune check-point inhibitor) , which is a overall thinking of the

combination treatments with drugs to reduce side effect.

Negative control (no treatment):

KM-curve: Poorest curve that has the steepest slope( which means many mice dead in a short amount of time).

Tumor size and density: Largest average tumor size and most tumors presented

Immunologic: Least number of lymphocytes and myeloid cells.

T cell activation: Least amount of fluorescence (least amount of activated T cell against tumor cell)

ICI (PD-1) only:

KM-curve: A better curve that has the flatter slope

Tumor size and density: smaller average tumor size and reduced number of tumors presented

Immunologic: More lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Increased amount of fluorescence

ICI with MAPK (RAS inhibitor):

KM-curve: A better curve that has a flatter slope than ICI only

Tumor size and density: smaller average tumor size and reduced number of tumors presented compared to ICI only

Immunologic: More lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Increased amount of fluorescence

ICI with the oncolytic virus (T-vec):

similar to ICI with MAPK

Triple combination (ICI + T-vec + kinase inhibitors):

KM-curve: The best curve (except positive control) that has the flattest curve

Tumor size and density: smallest average tumor size and least number of tumors presented compared to ICI only

Immunologic: Most lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Maximum amount of fluorescence

Positive control (no tumor): normal lifespan without injection

KM-curve: The curve shows the natural rate of death

Tumor size and density: shouldn’t present except spontaneous mutation

Immunologic: Normal lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Only few fluorescence (No pathogen, so no requirement to get activated)

Precondition: In the ideal state, the same number of the humanized mice in the four groups have both symptoms cause by ICIs.

*DCR (*disease control rate)

Explanation: 1 represents be controlled totally

​ 0 represents not be controlled

OSR (overall survival rate)

negative control:

DCR-curve not be controlled

OS-curve: Poorest curve that has the steepest slope

irAE related symptoms and biomarkers: Most severe

inflammation symptoms and cytokine concentration: Most severe and Highest

triple combination with anti-IL6 antibodies:

DCR-curve: be partly controlled

OS-curve: A better curve that has the flatter slope

(note: anti-cytokine antibodies seems to suppress the therapeutic effect of triple combination)

irAE related symptoms and biomarkers: Less severe

inflammation symptoms and cytokine concentration: Less severe and Lower

triple combination with corticosteroid:

DCR-curve: be partly controlled

OS-curve: A better curve that has the flatter slope

irAE related symptoms and biomarkers: Less severe (a bit more severe than anti-IL6 antibodies)

inflammation symptoms and cytokine concentration: Less severe and Lower (more reduced effect than anti-IL6 antibodies)

triple combination with both side effect reducing drug:

DCR-curve: be well controlled

OS-curve: A better curve that has the flatter slope

irAE related symptoms and biomarkers: Least severe (except positive control)

inflammation symptoms and cytokine concentration: Least severe, and lowest cytokine concentration.

positive control:

DCR-curve: do not need to control

OS-curve: normal life and death rate
