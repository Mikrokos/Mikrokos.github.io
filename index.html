读后感

有关《乌合之众》的感想（一）
因为英语水平有限，此系列暂时不用英语来完成，等水平达到一定程度，会自行用双语模式进行。
每个想学心理学或是对这门学科感兴趣的人都或多或少听过读过《乌合之众》这部著作，它在心理学上几乎封神，知乎也每每推荐它作为人生必读的一百本小说去提高自身素养。稍微了解一点的人就会知道，《乌合之众》讲的主要是大众心理学，就是群体的思想。
当我带着对这本著作的好奇与期待翻开它的时候，我就意识到首要问题是我不会读。不是不认识字，也不是看不懂内容，而是，读不进去。
当你拿着荧光笔企图通过划重点的方式去提炼并作笔记时，你就会发现，通篇好像都是大道理，每一行好像都有有用的话，让你不知所措。那时我高一，深刻的意识到了读富含专业性的著作，要总结出阅读方法。
本篇感想就是想谈一谈我的阅读方法，以及收获的效果。
在淘宝上买的东西总会有赠品，比如那次《乌合之众》，赠送一张思维导图，当你仔细看图上的内容时就会发现，它提供了阅读脉络。首先分为五大类，从五个方面解释群众心理，然后每大类中有对应的总体观点，每个观点有若干条具体内容。看到这儿，好像有那么回事了，至少对每一章要说什么有了大概的认识，现在，可以开始正式阅读了。
再者的问题就是，怎么算读懂了？比如“群体受无意识支配。”应该是常被引用到的观点，当我读到这句话时，我会从记忆库中提取，问自已有没有能解释的事例，也就是往自己身上代入，我在一个群体中做的事是不是根据自己的想法。显然，有的时候不是，当老师不在教室时，我会跟其他人一起吵闹。再深入一点想想有没有生活中听到或看到过的事件。当然也有，应该是很火的视频，突然有人在列车车厢中开始跑，其他人就会跟着跑，场面非常混乱。这就是典型的无意识行为，在不知发生什么时人们会随大流。
现在又有新的问题了，通俗的说随大流的话，谁都懂，好像就是换个高深的说法。我个人感觉，从某种程度上确实是，就是把很常见的人类行为拿出来解释，探讨是什么促使人们做出相应的反应，但是在寻找的过程，应该会相当有趣。
然后，当你能够想清楚每个观点时，再去看看作者的解释，就能算略知一二了。专业性强的作品的不同之处就是它没有故事情节，剧情不连贯，没有高潮。但很多平淡的作品也很有可读性是因为有感情。以上二者皆不具有的就要使用技巧，先看进去，再谈其他内容。

Goals in life

Everybody tries to achieve a goal in their lives. I remember that I once set a goal for myself to pass the 10th grade of piano. I saw it as the final objective in my process of studying the piano. In addition, it was a little challenging but well worth trying..

I spent six months preparing for the test. This period lasted from the spring semester to the summer vacation. Moreover, I found it quite difficult to keep practicing playing the piano for over 8 hours a day while others were enjoying their holiday. It seems that I spent most of my day practicing piano except the time that I had meals and slept. I sometimes felt bored, however I always told myself ‘ No pain no gain.’.

Furthermore, my piano teacher helped me a lot. She was strict enough to point out my errors, even minor things like the wrong rhythm which I did not care about it. She encouraged me when I felt unsatisfied with the music I played. Also, I watched some videos which were published by some famous pianist. At the same time, I tried to learn from them in order to make my playing vivid and hopefully impress the examiner so that I get a good score. They also played an important role as a guide to show me the direction I should head in.

Ultimately, my hard-working helped me to achieve my goal. I got the highest score in the exam. I was proud to get the result, knowing I had been through so much hard practice to get there.

Overall, I enjoyed the preparing for the exam. I particularly liked to guess the thinking of the authors. In addition, I was keeping trying to show the picture at that moment by playing the music in different ways. As long as I try my best to achieve my goal, no matter what the final result is, I will not have any regrets at that moment.

IM

Acute Infectious Mononucleosis

Context

Background& Brief introduction

Case study

More information

■Monocytosis is an increase in the number of monocytes circulating . Monocytes are white blood cells that give rise to macrophages and dendritic cells in the immune system.

■Infectious mononucleosis (IM, mono), also known as glandular fever, is an infection usually caused by the Epstein–Barr virus (EBV).

（A picture about mono）

MHC class I

CD8 cytotoxic T cells kill infected cells by recognizing foreign pathogen derived peptides that are transported to the cell surface bound to MHC class I molecules. The peptides carried by MHC class I molecules come from the degradation of proteins in the cytosol, and so cytotoxic T cells act against pathogens whose proteins are found in the cytosol of the host cell at some stage in their life cycle.

Cytotoxic T cells kill their infected targets with great precision and neatness, by inducing apoptosis in the infected cell while sparing adjacent normal cells; this strategy minimizes tissue damage .

CD8 cytotoxic T cells release two types of preformed cytotoxins which seem able to induce apoptosis in any type of target cell, and the protein perforin, which is thought to act as a translocator, pore-forming protein to enable granzymes to cross the membrane of the target cell.

EBV (also known as human herpesvirus 4) is a member of the virus family Herpesviridae. It has a double-stranded linear DNA genome enclosed in an icosahedral capsid and a lipid envelope, it replicates its DNA genome in the host -cell nucleus. EBV infects only humans and is one of the most successful infectious agents on its obligate host. The virus infects mainly B cells and epithelial cells.

（EBV under magnification）

Analysis of single EBV-infected cells from tonsils of IM patients for rearranged immunoglobulin genes revealed two strategies of EBV for rapid and massive spread in the B cell compartment:

The direct infection of many naive as well as memory cell.

Germinal center B cells and the expansion of the latter cells to large clones.

Infected B cells are stimulated to secrete immunoglobulin, producing, among other antibodies whose detection is one of the most widely used diagnostic tests for EBV infection. This heterophile antibody is not specific for EBV antigens but binds to antigens present on heterologous red blood cells and agglutinates them.

Case study

(a bad sore throat from a B-cell infection)

Emma Bovary

15-year-old female with severe sore throat , fever and malaise.

Her throat was so swollen and she had difficulty swallowing.

Her anterior and posterior cervical lymph nodes were swollen and tender (lymphadenopathy).

Her abdomen felt soft and the liver was enlarged.

Emma’s 1-year-old brother

became ill at the same time, but did not have such severe symptoms. He was merely listless and felt warm. He had no particular physical symptoms, and seemed to recover completely after a few days.

Overall

clinical signs and symptoms

At least 3 of the following symptoms are positive:

fever

pharyngitis, tonsillitis

cervical lymph node enlargement (> 1cm)

the liver enlarged

Splenomegaly

Treatment

She received 1 liter of normal saline intravenously followed by 20 mg of methylprednisolone (a corticosteroid) intravenously every 12 hours. Her throat culture again proved negative for streptococcus but her blood serum was positive for lgM and lgG antibodies against EBV capsid antigen.

Emma improved quickly with the symptomatic treatment and was discharged on the second day after admission to complete her recovery at home.

Why the symptoms are different?

Answer

The signs and symptoms of infectious mononucleosis vary with age.

Children

Before puberty, the disease typically only produces flu-like symptoms, if any at all. When found, symptoms tend to be similar to those of common throat infections

Adolescents and young adults

In adolescence and young adulthood, the disease presents with a characteristic triad:

Fever

Sore throat

Swollen glands

combination treatments with drugs to reduce side effect.

Negative control (no treatment):

KM-curve: Poorest curve that has the steepest slope( which means many mice dead in a short amount of time).

Tumor size and density: Largest average tumor size and most tumors presented

Immunologic: Least number of lymphocytes and myeloid cells.

T cell activation: Least amount of fluorescence (least amount of activated T cell against tumor cell)

ICI (PD-1) only:

KM-curve: A better curve that has the flatter slope

Tumor size and density: smaller average tumor size and reduced number of tumors presented

Immunologic: More lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Increased amount of fluorescence

ICI with MAPK (RAS inhibitor):

KM-curve: A better curve that has a flatter slope than ICI only

Tumor size and density: smaller average tumor size and reduced number of tumors presented compared to ICI only

Immunologic: More lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Increased amount of fluorescence

ICI with the oncolytic virus (T-vec):

similar to ICI with MAPK

Triple combination (ICI + T-vec + kinase inhibitors):

KM-curve: The best curve (except positive control) that has the flattest curve

Tumor size and density: smallest average tumor size and least number of tumors presented compared to ICI only

Immunologic: Most lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Maximum amount of fluorescence

Positive control (no tumor): normal lifespan without injection

KM-curve: The curve shows the natural rate of death

Tumor size and density: shouldn’t present except spontaneous mutation

Immunologic: Normal lymphocytes and myeloid cells were presented.

T cell activation: Only few fluorescence (No pathogen, so no requirement to get activated)
