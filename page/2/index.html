Garbage classification

Since November 1, 2020, Nanjing has begun to enforce garbage classification, and residential areas have started to put garbage into designated places. It was expected that citizens would be dissatisfied with this regulation, but in fact, it did not make people complain like Shanghai before, and people accepted this regulation and actively carried out garbage classification.

So how should we practice garbage classification in schools?
First of all, it is natural to start from the individual. We should have a sense of classification in our hearts. For example, we should prepare garbage bags by ourselves, place them in a unified way, and clean the garbage at the garbage dropping point at a fixed time every day.

Second is to strengthen education, campus often waste generated in the limited range, is just some used waste paper, snack bags, cans, yogurt, milk cartons, if you do not know what these respectively belong to waste, can search, or use the class meeting to get to know, so that it can achieve the campus of garbage classification.

Moreover, the unique classification method of each class. Buying small sorting garbage cans is the most common method. In this way, in the process of throwing garbage, people’s classification consciousness can also be strengthened and thinking can be triggered.

Finally is to everyone’s initiative, separate garbage is not a person, is a collective, garbage sorting is easy, but you always worry about this matter, not a I literally throw that’s all right, anyway, nobody knows what is I throw, classification is a sanitation worker or the helper, if so, class’s and grade’s garbage classification must do bad, regular appraisals garbage classification can improve class honor outstanding class and the desire to do it well.

The causes of Rwandan genocide

The Rwandan genocide, also known as the genocide against the Tutsi, was a mass slaughter of Tutsi, Twa, and moderate Hutu in Rwanda, which took place between 7 April and 15 July 1994 during the Rwandan Civil War. (From Wikipedia)
We can first have a look at some background about the genocide. Back to the period after World war ll, revolution and Hutu–Tutsi relations after independence makes the two ethnic group had been in a state of confrontation for a long time, and then Rwandan Civil War happened in the 1980s, also at 1990, a pogrom was organised on 11 October in a commune in Gisenyi Province, killing 383 Tutsi.(From Wikipedia) It seems that the conflict between the Hutu and Tutsi become more and more serious. I considered this is the first cause.
After that, let us turn to the major cause, most of the experts think it is the immediate cause. On 6 April 1994, the airplane carrying Rwandan President Juvénal Habyarimana and Cyprien Ntaryamira, the Hutu president of Burundi, was shot down as it prepared to land in Kigali, killing everyone on board.(From Wikipedia) The genocidal killings finally began the next day. Soldiers, police, and militia quickly executed key Tutsi and moderate Hutu military, they also recruited and pressured Hutu civilians to arm themselves with machetes, clubs, blunt objects, and other weapons and encouraged them to rape, maim, and kill their Tutsi neighbors and to destroy or steal their property. (From Wikipedia) This was the main event of the Rwandan genocide. And I think it is the second cause, also the most recongnized. Someone think the attack was a premeditated murder.
All above are the internal reasons, that means it happened because of the country itself, I also find some other causes.

For example, why they had conflict? Maybe there is something different between the two ethnic groups. There were people whose religion, culture, habits are all different. However they had to live in the same area which could not make both two agree with the other. Another important reason was due to the United Nations. As we all know one of the duties is prevent fighting and violence from happening, but it did not finish the job well. The United Nations Assistance Mission for Rwanda (UNAMIR) had been in Rwanda since October 1993, with a mandate to oversee the implementation of the Arusha Accords. UNAMIR’s Chapter VI mandate rendered it powerless to intervene militarily, and most of its Rwandan staff were killed in the early days of the genocide, severely limiting its ability to operate. UNAMIR was therefore largely reduced to a bystander role, and Dallaire later labelled it a “failure”.(From Wikipedia)

And now we need to talk about the genocide in two concepts. One is the realism, which emphasizes competition and conflict in international relations. The state is the major role and also the justice and theoretical ethics.. Otherwise, liberalism emphasizes cooperation and morality in international relations. In this perspective, a country is a collection of different domestic social interest groups.

**The cause of the ethnic conflict can be contributed to the competition between the two different national in the same state. It involves national security interests and power disputes at the same time. The event turned violent because there was no peaceful solution.
